Privacy policy

CODYPASTE was made to make sharing your content (mostly code) with others much easier. Any public project you create is visible to anyone who visits CODYPASTE and it's automatically licensed under MIT license. You can read more about MIT license here: MIT License.
Because we are not collecting any of your data, we are not able to connect created project with specific website user so created projects CAN NOT be removed.
If you think that any uploaded project breaks law or violates copyright laws contact us here: Contact
Encryption
All projects stored using CODYPASTE are encrypted with key attached in URL. It means that we do not store original form of your projects, only its encrypted form.
Encryption was done using popular alghoritm called AES (more info can be found here)
Decoding of project source is being done live, whenever user enters into project URL with usage of key attached to it. Be aware, that only valid key will result in fully readable form of our snippet, any change in key will result in totally different project content.
Security
Private projects are secured with password you provided in submit form. We have attempted to protect CODYPASTE by implementing generally available security technologies like TLSv1.2.
These things prevents from unauthorized access, ensures secure connection with our servers, but no guarantee can be made that your information and data will be secure from intrusions and unauthorized release to third parties.
Cookies
We do not use cookies.
Local storage (more details here)
We use HTML5 local storage ONLY for storing information about your shared projects to make it easier for user to store their snippets. If you do not want to store any of those in your storage, you can easily remove them.
Cache storage (more details here)
We use cache storage (and Service Workers) to improve performance of our Website. You can disable cache in your browser settings.
Third parties
We do not sell or share anything with third parties. Keep in mind that public projects are visible to anyone having your URL with valid key so and you should consider content you share this way available for everyone.
What data CODYPASTE.io does NOT collect?
We do not collect your personal data. We do not store any cookies on your computer.
